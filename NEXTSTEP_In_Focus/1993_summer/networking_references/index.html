NEXTSTEP NETWORKING REFERENCES
Here are some useful sources of NetInfo and networking information. They are referenced throughout this issue.

Albitz, Paul, and Cricket Liu. DNS and BIND in a Nutshell. Sebastopol, CA: O'Reilly & Associates, 1992.

Comer, Douglas. Internetworking with TCP/IP, Volume I: Principles, Protocols, and Architecture (Second Edition). Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice Hall, 1991.

Computer Systems Research Group, University of California/Berkeley. UNIX Programmer's Reference Manual and System Maintenance Manual, 4.3 Berkeley Software Distribution, Virtual VAX-11 Version. Berkeley, CA: USENIX Association, 1986.

Cottle, Dave. "When One Server Isn't Enough." support bulletin 3 (Winter 1993): 19-31.

Cottle, Dave. "The Crash of the Master NetInfo Server." support bulletin 3 (Winter 1993): 39-43.

Garfinkel, Simson, and Gene Spafford. Practical UNIX Security. Sebastopol, CA: O'Reilly & Associates, 1991.

Eastman, P. D. Are You My Mother? New York: Random House, 1988.

Majka, Marc. "Behind the Scenes of NeXT Networking." support bulletin 2 (Summer 1992): 8-24.

Marcum, Alan M. "Relating NetInfo to Classic UNIX." support bulletin 1 (Winter 1991): 15-21.

Nemeth, Evi, Garth Snyder, and Scott Seebass. UNIX System Administration Handbook. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice Hall, 1989.

NeXT Computer, Inc. NEXTSTEP Network and System Administration. Menlo Park, CA: Addison-Wesley, 1992.

NeXT Computer, Inc. On-line files /etc/rc.boot, /etc/rc, and /etc/rc.net. NEXTSTEP Release 3.

NeXT Computer, Inc. Online UNIX manual pages. NEXTSTEP Release 3.

Stern, Hal. Managing NFS and NIS. Sebastopol, CA: O'Reilly & Associates, 1991.

Tucker, Lee. "Securing Your NeXT Computer on a WAN." support bulletin 2 (Spring 1992): 37-41.

-Alan M. Marcum
